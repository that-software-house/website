# TSH — Creative Outreach Strategies

> Cold email is table stakes. Everyone's doing it. Here are the approaches that actually get responses.

---

## Strategy 1: "See Before You Buy" — The Live Preview Drop

**The idea**: Before reaching out, spend 2–3 hours rebuilding their homepage as a modern redesign. Host it on a subdomain. Send them the link. That's it.

**Why it works**:
- Zero ambiguity about what they're getting
- You've already done work, which signals confidence and commitment
- The prospect's ego kicks in — they *want* that better version of themselves
- Eliminates the biggest objection in web design sales ("I don't know what I'll get")
- Conversion rate on this approach in the industry: **3–5x higher than cold email**

**How to execute**:

1. **Pick targets with visibly bad websites** — Run the prospect list through GTMetrix or PageSpeed Insights. Prioritize anyone scoring below 50 on mobile performance. That's your opening line.

2. **Build the preview** — Use their existing branding (logo, colors, copy) but fix everything: layout, typography, mobile responsiveness, loading speed, CTA placement. Host it at `preview.thatsoftwarehouse.com/[businessname]` or a dedicated subdomain.

3. **The outreach message** (keep it short, the link does the talking):

   > Subject: We built you something
   >
   > Hi [Dr. Name],
   >
   > We took your current site and rebuilt it the way it should look. No strings attached — just wanted to show you what's possible.
   >
   > [preview.thatsoftwarehouse.com/smileclinic-austin]
   >
   > If you like it, we can have the real thing live in 2 weeks. Starting at $1,000.
   >
   > — [Name], That Software House

4. **The follow-up**: If no response in 3 days, send a screenshot comparison side-by-side (before vs. after) as an image inline in the email. Visual diff is brutally effective.

**Resource cost per prospect**: ~3–4 hours of dev time
**Best for**: Dental practices, medical spas, local businesses with visibly outdated sites
**Expected conversion**: 15–25% of preview links sent will get a reply

## Strategy 2: Trigger-Based Gifting — "We Noticed You Just..."

**The idea**: Monitor trigger events that signal a company is in motion — raising money, opening a new location, winning an award, hiring a new partner. Strike when they're in an "investing in growth" mindset. Send a gift with a hyper-relevant message.

**This is NOT random gifting. The trigger IS the message.**

### Trigger Events & Gift Pairings

**For startups / SaaS / tech companies (TSH's custom software + AI clients):**

| Trigger Event | Gift | Message Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Seed/Series A raise announced | Bottle of champagne + handwritten note | "Congrats on the raise. You're about to move fast. So are we." |
| Just launched a product | Framed screenshot of their launch day + note | "We watched your launch. Here's what we'd build next." |
| New CTO/VP Eng hired | Curated book on engineering leadership | "New leader, new stack decisions. We should talk." |
| Featured in TechCrunch/press | Framed article print | "We saw the coverage. This is the moment to go faster." |
| Just hit 10k users milestone | Custom branded milestone trophy | "10k users means your infra is about to get tested. We can help." |

**For dental / healthcare practices (TSH's web + SEO clients):**

| Trigger Event | Gift | Message Angle |
|---|---|---|
| New practice opening | Succulent/plant with branded note | "Congrats on the new location. We'd love to build your digital front door." |
| Practice acquired / new owner | Bottle of wine + handwritten note | "New ownership is the perfect time for a new website. We can launch in 2 weeks." |
| Practice celebrating anniversary (10/20/25 yr) | Custom framed "est. year" print | "25 years of great dentistry. Your website should match." |
| Won a local "Best Of" award | Custom plaque/frame with their award | "You just won Best Dentist in Austin. Your website doesn't show it yet." |
| Hiring aggressively (seen on Indeed/LinkedIn) | — | "You're growing. Does your website reflect that?" |

### How to Find Trigger Events

- **Funding**: Crunchbase, TechCrunch, LinkedIn announcements — free alerts via Google Alerts on "[city] startup funding"
- **New dental practice**: Google Maps "recently opened" filter, local chamber of commerce new member announcements
- **Practice for sale / acquisition**: Practice brokerage sites (wingspantransitions.com, triumphant transition partners) — new owners are a goldmine
- **Awards**: Local business journals publish "Best Of" lists annually (Austin Business Journal, Dallas Morning News, Houston Chronicle)
- **Anniversaries**: Google "[practice name] founded" or check their About page
- **Hiring signals**: Set Indeed/LinkedIn job alerts for "dental receptionist Austin" — a practice posting multiple jobs is growing and has budget

### Execution

1. Set up Google Alerts for: `"Austin" "dental practice" "new location"` and similar
2. Use Apollo.io or LinkedIn Sales Navigator for startup funding triggers
3. Keep a gift inventory stocked: champagne (×10), succulents (×20), handwritten card supply
4. Assign one person to monitor triggers 30 min/week
5. Ship gifts via Sendoso, Reachdesk, or manually via local delivery

**Resource cost**: $30–$100 per gift + shipping
**Best for**: High-value targets (custom software clients, new practice owners)
**Expected conversion**: 30–40% response rate on gifted outreach (industry benchmark)

## Strategy 3: The "Your Competitor Just Upgraded" Alert

**The idea**: Monitor when a competitor of your prospect launches a new website. Reach out to the prospect immediately with a direct comparison.

**Why it works**: Nothing motivates a business owner faster than seeing their direct competitor look better than them. It's competitive psychology, and it's completely ethical.

**How to execute**:

1. Build a list of dental practices in each target neighborhood (e.g., all dentists within 2 miles of each other in Austin)
2. When one of them launches a new site (you'll see this via BuiltWith change alerts or Visualping monitoring), immediately reach out to the other 3–4 in the same area
3. The message:

   > Subject: [Competitor Name] just upgraded their website
   >
   > Hi [Dr. Name],
   >
   > Just noticed that [Smiles on Congress] launched a new website this week. It looks great and loads fast on mobile.
   >
   > When a competitor in your area upgrades, it affects your Google ranking and first impressions. Thought you'd want to know.
   >
   > We built their site. Happy to do the same for you — we can have something live in 2 weeks. Want to see a preview?
   >
   > — [Name], That Software House

**Tools**: Visualping.io (monitor competitor websites for changes), BuiltWith (detect new CMS installs)
**Best for**: Dental practices, medical spas in competitive local markets (Austin, Dallas, Houston)
**Expected conversion**: 10–20% — the scarcity/FOMO angle is real

## Strategy 4: The Video Audit Bomb

**The idea**: Record a 3–5 minute personalized Loom video walking through their current website, narrating specific issues out loud. No slides, no pitch. Just screen share + your voice pointing at problems.

**Why it works**:
- It feels human, not automated
- Showing > telling — seeing someone scroll their actual site while talking is visceral
- It demonstrates expertise before asking for anything
- Loom videos have 4–6x higher open/engagement rates than plain text emails
- The prospect feels like you already know their business

**Script structure**:
1. "Hey [Name], I'm [X] from That Software House — spent 5 minutes on your site this morning..."
2. Show the homepage on mobile — point out load time, layout breaks
3. Show what Google search results look like for their target keywords — they're not on page 1
4. Show one competitor's site that looks better
5. "Here's what I'd change first..." — give them 2–3 specific, actionable things
6. End: "We can fix all of this in 2 weeks. Starting at $1,000. Reply if you want to see a preview."

**Tools**: Loom (free tier works), Vidyard
**Time per video**: 20–30 min (including recording + light edit)
**Best for**: Mid-priority prospects where you want personalization without building a full preview site
**Expected conversion**: 20–30% video open rate, 8–15% reply rate

## Strategy 5: The "Free Audit" Content Play (Inbound Trigger)

**The idea**: Publish a free "Website & SEO Health Check for Dental Practices" tool on TSH's website. Drive traffic via LinkedIn posts and local dentist Facebook groups. Warm leads come to you.

**How it works**:
1. Build a simple form: "Enter your practice website URL + email"
2. Run automated checks: PageSpeed score, mobile score, Google Maps ranking, number of reviews
3. Send a branded PDF report within 60 seconds
4. Follow up manually within 24 hours with the Loom video audit (Strategy 4) for anyone who scores below 70

**Why dental practices will use it**:
- It's free
- It's specific to their industry (not a generic web checker)
- It speaks their language ("Are new patients finding you on Google?")
- The report itself is the sales pitch — every low score is a problem TSH can solve

**Distribution**:
- Post in local dentist Facebook groups (search "Austin dentist group," "Texas dental professionals")
- LinkedIn article: "5 Reasons Your Dental Practice Website is Losing You Patients (Free Check)"
- Partner with dental CPAs, practice consultants, dental supply reps who can share with their clients

**Build time**: 1–2 days to build the audit tool + PDF generator (TSH can build this itself)
**Ongoing cost**: Near-zero
**Expected output**: 5–20 warm leads per month from inbound

## Strategy 6: Hyper-Local "Just Down the Street" Approach

**The idea**: Since TSH is in Austin, walk into local dental practices, medical spas, and small businesses physically. Leave a printed one-pager showing their current website performance and a "before/after" mockup.

**Why it works**:
- Nobody does this anymore
- It's impossible to ignore
- Local business owners respect other local businesses
- Austin's "buy local" culture plays in your favor
- No inbox required — zero competition

**The leave-behind**:
- A4 or letter-sized printed card, high quality paper
- Left side: Screenshot of their current site with 3 red arrows pointing at problems
- Right side: "What it could look like" mockup or just clean stats (your PageSpeed: 34/100 | Your competitor's: 87/100)
- Bottom: "We're around the corner at [TSH address]. Starting at $1,000. thatsoftwarehouse.com"

**Pair with**: Drop off at 10 practices in one neighborhood in a single afternoon. Mention you're local. Ask for the owner by name.

## Putting It Together: The Recommended Sequence

For maximum conversion, layer these strategies rather than picking one:

```
Week 0:  Set up trigger monitoring (Google Alerts, Visualping, Crunchbase)
Week 1:  Identify 10 targets with worst websites → Build previews for top 3
Week 1:  Send "See Before You Buy" preview links to top 3
Week 1:  Record Loom videos for next 7 targets
Week 2:  Send Loom video emails to 7 targets
Week 2:  Follow up preview targets with side-by-side screenshot
Week 3:  Trigger event fires → gift goes out to 1–2 high-value targets
Week 4:  Walk-in visits to 5 Austin local businesses
Ongoing: Free audit tool drives inbound → warm follow-ups
```

**Monthly cost estimate**:
- Preview site builds (3/month): ~12 hrs dev time
- Loom videos (7/month): ~3.5 hrs
- Gifts (2/month): ~$150
- Walk-in materials (print): ~$30
- Total: ~$200/month + ~16 hrs of time

**Expected monthly output from this stack**:
- 3–5 closed web packages ($1,000–$5,000 each)
- 1–2 SEO retainers ($1,000–$2,500/month)
- 1 custom software conversation every 6–8 weeks
